Target Groups & Audiences

1
NFU County Branches (Norfolk, Suffolk, Cambs)
Email county secretaries directly. Every branch has 200-500 members. Ask to be included in their WhatsApp/email circular. Personal introduction, not a cold pitch.
2
Facebook Farming Groups
East Anglian Farming, Norfolk Farming, Suffolk Farmers, Cambridgeshire Arable, UK Grain Markets. Share highest-value posts (markets, schemes) 2x per week max. The page must be a member first. Lead with data, not promotion.
3
Cheffins Auction Days
QR code card at machinery auctions. You already pull their data — natural conversation starter. "We include your listings in our free weekly briefing."
4
Young Farmers Clubs (Norfolk, Suffolk, Cambs YFC)
County federations are digitally active. Offer to present at a meeting. "10 data sources, one email, every Monday — free."
5
LinkedIn Agronomists
Agronomists serve 20-50 farmers each. One convert shares with their whole client list. Post the grain price table on LinkedIn weekly with #ukfarming.
6
Agricultural Colleges (Easton, Writtle, Shuttleworth)
Contact lecturers directly. Useful teaching resource. Students become future subscribers and share with farming families.
7
Norfolk & Suffolk Shows
Badge/card presence at Royal Norfolk Show, Suffolk Show, Hadleigh Show, Tendring Show. QR code to sign-up. "Your weekly briefing, from the data that matters."

Messaging Strategy

  • Lead with data, not promotion. Every post starts with a verified number. The newsletter CTA comes at the end, in the comments — never the headline.
  • The maths speaks. "On a 500t sale, that's £2,450 more than last week" is more powerful than "prices are up."
  • Credibility through sources. Always cite AHDB, Met Office, GOV.UK, Defra. Farmers trust what they can verify.
  • Local specificity wins. "Eastern region ex-farm" beats "UK average." "Norwich Livestock Market" beats "cattle prices."
  • Never advise. Report what the data says. Let the farmer draw their own conclusions. "Your farm, your call" is the unspoken principle.
  • Share hooks on high-value posts. "Worth forwarding to anyone with uncontracted old crop" — specific, useful, not generic "tag a farmer."
